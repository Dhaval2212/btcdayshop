What is Bitcoin?

A Decentralized Asset

Bitcoin is the world's first decentralized digital currency. It operates without a central bank or single administrator, using a peer-to-peer network (the blockchain) to verify transactions.

Satoshi Nakamoto

Released in 2009 by an anonymous individual or group known as Satoshi Nakamoto, Bitcoin was created as a response to the 2008 financial crisis, aiming for a system independent of traditional banking.

Limited Supply

Only 21 million Bitcoins will ever exist. This scarcity is hardcoded into the protocol, making it a "deflationary" asset, often compared to gold.

How It Works

The Blockchain

A public ledger that records all transactions. Blocks of data are linked using cryptography, making it nearly impossible to alter historical records.

Proof of Work

Bitcoin uses a "Proof of Work" (PoW) consensus mechanism. Miners solve complex mathematical puzzles to secure the network and are rewarded with newly minted BTC.

Privacy & Security

Transactions are pseudonymised—linked to addresses rather than identities. High-level encryption ensures that only the holder of a private key can access their funds.
